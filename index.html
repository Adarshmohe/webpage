Indore Transport system

Indore's transport system primarily relies on road-based options including city buses (some equipped with GPS and IVR), private minibuses,
and various other modes like tempos (replaced by Maruti vans), Metro taxis, and auto-rickshaws,
with plans to introduce a double-decker bus service and explore ropeway solutions.

History :

Indore's transportation history, featuring public buses, radio taxis, and e-bikes, began with the incorporation of Indore City Transport Services Limited in 2005, a PPP scheme operating under Atal Indore City Transport Services Ltd, now operating on 36 routes and 170 bus stops.
